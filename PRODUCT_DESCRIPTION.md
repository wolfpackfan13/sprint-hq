# SprintHQ — Product Description

## One-liner
SprintHQ is a personal command center that briefs you and executes — built to keep a multi-business operator on track without becoming one more thing to maintain.

## The problem it solves
When you run several ventures at once, the hardest part isn't doing the work — it's holding *what matters right now* in your head. Priorities slip out of sight, the wrong thing gets worked on, and important threads die in the gap between "out of mind" and "on the calendar." SprintHQ is the external brain that carries that memory: it decides what deserves attention, surfaces what's going neglected, and puts the right work in front of you.

## Who it's for
A self-employed entrepreneur running multiple businesses — a housing fund, real estate flips, a service business, a personal brand — who thinks in systems, wants control over outcomes, and needs structure delivered fast and direct. Built specifically for a decisive, results-driven operator with ADHD who wants to delegate the busywork but keep every decision.

## Design principles
1. **Command, don't maintain.** Every feature answers "what's the state, and what do I do next?" in as few taps as possible. The app reports to you; you don't babysit it.
2. **Do more, show less.** Capability without clutter. A tight, predictable surface beats a feature maze.
3. **Client/Area is the unit.** Work is tracked by area of business, not by individual contact. The system flags an area that's gone quiet while work is still open — so nothing gets neglected.
4. **AI proposes, you approve.** Intelligence acts like a chief of staff: it drafts, suggests, and organizes the busywork, but never takes a real action without your sign-off.
5. **One source of connective tissue.** Documents live in your existing tools (Google Drive); SprintHQ holds the running narrative — notes, decisions, action items, history, and what's next — and links the documents in.

## What it does
- **Plans your day and surfaces priorities** — a focused Today view (Top 3, what's missed, what's unscheduled) plus a full task pipeline grouped by time horizon so nothing hides.
- **Organizes work by client/area and project** — drill from an area into its open loops, timeline, projects, and meetings; drill from a project into its tasks; filter the whole system to one client.
- **Tracks time and bills for it** — start a timer from anywhere, assign it as you go, and roll hours up by client into PDF invoices.
- **Captures meetings into action** — meeting notes and action items become real tasks, filed to the right area automatically.
- **Connects to your calendar and inbox** — views your schedule, proposes prep tasks from meetings, and (on the roadmap) time-blocks your work and turns email into approved tasks and drafts.
- **Keeps the long view** — a 12-week sprint frame, weekly reviews, goals, and a searchable archive of everything completed.

## Tone & experience
Sharp, calm, and fast. Light, focused interface with restrained accents (gold reserved for current focus, green for done). Centered, readable columns instead of sprawling layouts. Direct language, minimal ceremony, momentum over friction.

## What it is not
- Not a team collaboration suite — it's a personal operating system for one operator.
- Not a document store — it points to Google Drive for files.
- Not an autopilot — AI assists and proposes; the operator stays in command.
